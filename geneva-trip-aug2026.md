## Day-by-day

### Sat 8 Aug — Arrival (low energy, jet-lagged with an infant)
- Land ~07:50, but hotel check-in is usually 15:00 — **request early check-in** in advance, or at least drop bags + freshen up.
- Keep it flat and walkable: **Old Town (Vieille Ville)**, **St. Pierre Cathedral**, **Parc des Bastions** (big chess boards, easy with a stroller).
- Lakefront: **Jet d'Eau**, **Jardin Anglais** and the flower clock (L'horloge fleurie).
- Early dinner, early night. Don't fight the jet lag.

### Sun 9 Aug — Day trip option (pick one)
- **Annecy, France (~45 min by car/bus)** — *easiest with an infant.* Storybook canal town, lakeside, very stroller-friendly. Relaxed.
- **Montreux + Château de Chillon (~1 hr by train)** — lakeside promenade, lake-edge castle. Scenic and gentle.
- **Chamonix / Mont Blanc (~1–1.5 hr)** — most dramatic, but the Aiguille du Midi cable car goes to 3,842 m; **altitude is not suitable for an infant**. Do the valley/village instead if you go, skip the high cable car.
- **Lausanne (~40 min by train)** — Ouchy lakefront, Olympic Museum gardens.

### Mon–Fri 10–14 Aug — Your work week

- Many Geneva museums are free; the **CGN lake boats** (Mouettes water taxis) are a cheap, fun ride across the lake.
- **Evenings:** family dinners together — see meal budget note below.

### Sat 15 Aug — Second day trip

## Meal subsidy — how far it goes

Per day the subsidy is **SGD 150** (30 + 60 + 60). Over 8–16 Aug (9 days) that's **~SGD 1,350**.

Rough conversion: **1 CHF ≈ 1.6 SGD**, so:
- Breakfast SGD 30 ≈ **~19 CHF** — fine (or just use the hotel breakfast if included).
- Lunch / dinner SGD 60 ≈ **~37 CHF each** — workable but **tight for Geneva**, which is one of the world's most expensive cities. A sit-down dinner main alone is often 30–45 CHF.

Tips to stretch it:
- The subsidy most likely covers **you (the employee)** only — assume Peilih's and Owen's meals are out of pocket unless your company says otherwise. **Confirm with your company** whether it's per-person or employee-only, and whether you need itemised receipts.
- Lunch is far cheaper than dinner in Switzerland — many restaurants do a **plat du jour** (daily lunch special) for ~20–25 CHF. Eat your bigger meal at lunch.
- Supermarkets (**Migros, Coop**) and their in-store cafeterias are excellent value for an infant-paced day.
- Keep all receipts in one place (photo each one) for the expense claim.

## Travelling with Owen (infant) — practical notes

- **Lap infant:** confirm the infant fare is ticketed; request a **bassinet/bulkhead** seat for the long-haul legs (limited, book early). The bassinet will be at Peilih's Business seat or your Economy seat depending on aircraft — coordinate at booking.
- **Stroller:** gate-check it; Geneva and the lakefront/Old Town are mostly stroller-friendly (Old Town has some cobbles/slopes).
- **Documents:** Owen needs his **own passport**; check it's valid 6+ months beyond travel. Switzerland is **Schengen** — confirm visa requirements for your nationalities well ahead.
- **Altitude:** avoid high cable cars (Aiguille du Midi, high Alpine peaks) with an infant.
- Pack for **cool mornings/evenings** even in August by the lake and mountains.
